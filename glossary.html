Glossary

Essential vocabulary

TERM DEFINITION
Biodiversity The variety of plant and animal life in a particular habitat or ecosystem.
 Carbon footprint The total amount of greenhouse gases produced to directly and indirectly support human activities.
Greenwashing  When a company gives a false impression of how its products are more environmentally sound than they actually are.
Landfield  A site for the disposal of waste materials by burial. It is the oldest and most common form of waste disposal.
Renewable energy  Energy from a source that is not depleted when used, such as wind or solar power..
Sustainability The ability to be maintained at a certain rate or level without depleting natural resources for future generations.
Wildfires  Large, destructive fires that spread quickly over woodland or brush, often exacerbated by climate change.
Energy-efficient  Using less energy to provide the same level of performance, comfort, or convenience.
To phase out  To gradually stop using something. (e.g., The government aims to phase out single-use plastics).
Wind turbine A wind-driven turbine for generating electricity
Solar pannel A device that changes energy from the sun into electricity:
Eco-friendly  Not harmful to the environment; designed to have little or no damaging effect on the ecosystem.
